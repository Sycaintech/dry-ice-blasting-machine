Frequently Asked Questions

Which Materials Can Withstand Dry Ice Blasting?

Dry ice blasting is ideal for metals, rubber, concrete, and wood, cleaning effectively without damage. For softer materials like thin metals, plastics, electronics, or glass, adjust pressure and test on a less critical object first to avoid damage.

Is an External Compressed-air Source Necessary?

Yes. The dry ice blaster must be connected to an external compressed-air source with the following data: Compressed Air Input Pressure: 87 ~116 psi (Adjustable) and horse power: ≥7.5Kw (10HP). Dry Ice Output: 0.66 lb/min ~ 1.32 lb/min, depending on nozzle combination.

Is Dismantling Necessary Before Dry Ice Blasting?

It is often unnecessary to dismantle machinery or equipment to be cleaned by dry ice blasting because the process is completely dry and leaves no blasting material or chemical residues.

What is the ROI of dry ice blasting?

Most customers see ROI within 6-12 months through reduced labor costs, eliminated disposal fees, decreased downtime, and improved cleaning efficiency.
